About me

I build, test, and find solutions.

I'm a mechanical engineering student at UC Berkeley interested in autonomous systems, product design, vehicles, fabrication, and electromechanical projects.

01

My Path

I grew up in the South Bay and began my college journey at community college before transferring to UC Berkeley. I'm now approaching my senior year in mechanical engineering.

Taking the community college route taught me to be resourceful and intentional about the opportunities I pursue. Transferring to Berkeley gave me the chance to expand those skills through research, coursework, teaching, student organizations and increasingly complex design projects.

02

Building Things

I’ve always loved building and fixing things. Growing up, helping my dad with hands-on projects taught me to measure twice, learn from mistakes, and see things through to the end. That same mindset drives my engineering work today—whether I’m restoring classic cars, designing hardware for autonomous sailboats, or building custom furniture for my community.

03

Community

My Persian background is an important part of who I am and how I connect with others. At Berkeley, I serve as the Finance Director for ISCO, where I help support the organization’s events, operations, and community-building efforts.

Being involved with ISCO has allowed me to contribute outside the classroom while staying connected to Persian culture and helping create a welcoming space for other students.
