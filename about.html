About me

I build, test, repair, and improve things.

I am a mechanical engineering student at UC Berkeley interested in autonomous systems, product design, vehicles, fabrication, and electromechanical projects.

01

My Path

I grew up in the South Bay and began my college journey at community college before transferring to the University of California, Berkeley. I am now approaching my senior year in mechanical engineering.

Taking the community college route taught me to be resourceful, adaptable, and intentional about the opportunities I pursue. Transferring to Berkeley gave me the chance to expand those skills through engineering research, UAV coursework, teaching, student organizations, and increasingly complex design projects.

02

Building Things

I have always enjoyed making and repairing things. Some of my earliest experiences came from helping my dad with projects around the house and yard, where I learned to measure twice, accept mistakes, and finish what I started.

That mindset continues to shape how I approach engineering. My work has included restoring classic cars, converting a bicycle into an e-bike, designing renewable-energy hardware for autonomous sailboats, building a location-tracking clock, and creating furniture for my community.

03

Community

My Persian background is an important part of who I am and how I connect with others. At Berkeley, I serve as the Finance Director for ISCO, where I help support the organization’s events, operations, and community-building efforts.

Being involved with ISCO has allowed me to contribute outside the classroom while staying connected to Persian culture and helping create a welcoming space for other students.

04

Outside Engineering

When I am not working on a project, I enjoy sports, staying active, exploring new places, and spending time with friends and family. I like activities that keep me moving and give me a break from sitting behind a computer.

I am also a big food person. I enjoy trying new restaurants, discovering different cuisines, and finding places people might overlook. For me, food is one of the best ways to experience a culture and bring people together.
